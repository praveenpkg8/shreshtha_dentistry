**Fluoride Varnish: Benefits, Procedure & Aftercare**

Fluoride varnish is a quick, safe, and affordable treatment that helps prevent tooth decay in both children and adults. It strengthens enamel and protects teeth from cavities.

**What Is Fluoride Varnish?**

Fluoride varnish is a concentrated fluoride coating painted onto the teeth by a dental professional. It hardens quickly with saliva and stays on the teeth longer for better protection.

**How It Works**

Fluoride varnish:
✔ Strengthens enamel
✔ Remineralizes weak areas
✔ Slows cavity-causing bacteria
✔ Helps stop early decay

**Benefits**

- Prevents cavities
- Safe for toddlers, children, and adults
- Quick and painless (takes only minutes)
- Reduces tooth sensitivity
- Cost-effective preventive care

**Who Should Get It?**

Recommended for:

- Children and teens
- Adults prone to cavities
- Patients with braces or dry mouth
- Those with gum recession or frequent decay
Usually applied every **3–6 months**, depending on cavity risk.

**The Procedure**

1. Teeth are dried
1. Varnish is painted on
1. It hardens quickly
You can leave immediately after treatment.

**Aftercare Tips**

- Avoid hard or hot foods for 4–6 hours
- Eat soft foods
- Avoid brushing until the next morning (or as advised)
- Slight stickiness is normal and temporary

**Is It Safe?**

Yes. Fluoride varnish is widely used and safe for both children and adults. Only a small amount is applied, and it hardens quickly to minimize swallowing.
