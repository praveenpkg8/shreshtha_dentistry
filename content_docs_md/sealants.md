**Dental Sealants: Protecting Teeth from Decay**

Tooth decay is common in both children and adults. Dental sealants offer a simple, safe, and highly effective way to prevent cavities—especially on the chewing surfaces of back teeth.

**What ****Are**** Pit & Fissure Sealants?**

Sealants are thin protective coatings applied to the grooves of molars and premolars. These teeth have deep pits and fissures where food and bacteria easily get trapped.

Sealants act as a barrier, sealing these grooves and preventing decay.

**Why Molars Need Extra Protection**

Molars:

- Have deep, hard-to-clean grooves
- Trap food easily
- Are difficult for children to brush properly
Sealants smooth these surfaces, reducing cavity risk by up to **80%**.

**Benefits of Sealants**

✔ Highly effective cavity prevention
✔ Painless and non-invasive (no drilling)
✔ Quick application (minutes per tooth)
✔ Long-lasting protection (3–10 years)
✔ Cost-effective preventive care

**Who Should Get Sealants?**

- Children when permanent molars erupt (around ages 6 & 12)
- Teens prone to cavities
- Adults with deep grooves and no decay
- Patients at high cavity risk
Your dentist will determine if sealants are suitable.

**The Procedure**

1. Tooth cleaning
1. Drying the surface
1. Applying bonding solution
1. Painting sealant onto grooves
1. Hardening with a curing light
The process is quick and comfortable.

**Are Sealants Safe?**

Yes. Dental sealants are widely used and considered safe. They provide added protection but do not replace brushing and flossing.
