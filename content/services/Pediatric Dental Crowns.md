**Pediatric**** Dental Crowns: Protecting Your Child’s Smile**

When a baby tooth is badly decayed or damaged, a filling may not be enough. **Pediatric**** dental crowns** are tooth-shaped caps placed over primary teeth to restore strength, function, and appearance.

**When Are Crowns Needed?**

A child may need a crown if they have:

- Large cavities
- Tooth fractures
- After pulp therapy (baby tooth root canal)
- Weak enamel or high cavity risk
**Why Save Baby Teeth?**

Baby teeth help with:

- Proper chewing
- Clear speech
- Maintaining space for permanent teeth
- Guiding jaw growth
Losing them too early can cause alignment issues later.

**Types of ****Pediatric**** Crowns**

- **Stainless Steel Crowns (SSCs):** Durable, cost-effective, ideal for back teeth.
- **Zirconia Crowns:** Tooth-colored, metal-free, aesthetic option.
- **Strip Crowns:** Natural-looking composite crowns for front teeth.
**Benefits**

- Full coverage protection
- Strong and long-lasting
- Protect teeth after pulp therapy
- Improve chewing and appearance
- Often last until the baby tooth falls out naturally
**Procedure**

The process is quick and child-friendly:

1. Numbing the tooth
1. Removing decay
1. Shaping the tooth
1. Placing and cementing the crown
**Aftercare**

- Avoid sticky foods
- Maintain good brushing habits
- Attend regular dental checkups
**Pediatric**** crowns are safe, durable, and an excellent long-term solution for severely damaged baby teeth.**
