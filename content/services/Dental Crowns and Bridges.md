**Dental Crowns and Bridges: Restore Your Smile and Strengthen Your Teeth**

Damaged or missing teeth can affect your confidence, chewing ability, and overall oral health. **Dental crowns and bridges** are reliable, long-lasting solutions that restore both function and aesthetics.

**What Is a Dental Crown?**

A **dental crown** is a tooth-shaped cap placed over a weak or damaged tooth to restore its strength, shape, and appearance.

Crowns are recommended for:

- Severely decayed or cracked teeth
- After root canal treatment
- Large fillings
- Cosmetic smile enhancement
- Dental implant restoration
With proper care, crowns can last many years.

**Types of Crowns**

- **Zirconia** – Strong and aesthetic
- **PFM (Porcelain-Fused-to-Metal)** – Durable and affordable

**What Is a Dental Bridge?**

A **dental bridge** replaces one or more missing teeth using artificial teeth supported by adjacent teeth or implants.

Bridges help:

- Restore chewing function
- Prevent teeth shifting
- Improve speech
- Maintain facial structure
- Enhance smile appearance

**Procedure Overview**

1. Consultation and X-rays
1. Tooth preparation
1. Impression and temporary crown/bridge
1. Final placement of the custom restoration
Modern digital dentistry ensures accurate, comfortable, and natural-looking results.

**Benefits of Crowns and Bridges**

✔ Restore damaged teeth
✔ Replace missing teeth
✔ Improve chewing efficiency
✔ Enhance smile aesthetics
✔ Durable, long-term solution

**Are They Painful?**

The procedure is performed under local anesthesia and is generally pain-free. Mild sensitivity afterward is temporary.

**How Long Do They Last?**

With good oral hygiene and regular dental check-ups, **dental crowns and bridges typically last 10–15 years or longer**.
