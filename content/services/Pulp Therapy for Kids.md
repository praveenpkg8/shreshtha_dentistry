**Pulp Therapy for Kids: Saving Baby Teeth**

If your child has severe tooth pain or a deep cavity, a pediatric dentist may recommend **pulp therapy**. This safe and gentle treatment removes infection inside the tooth and helps save the baby tooth until it naturally falls out.

**What Is Pulp Therapy?**

Inside every tooth is soft tissue called the **pulp**, which contains nerves and blood vessels. When decay reaches this area, it can cause pain and infection.

Instead of removing the tooth, pulp therapy cleans the infection and restores the tooth. It’s similar to a root canal but specially designed for baby teeth.

**Why Save Baby Teeth?**

Baby teeth are important because they:

- Help children chew and speak properly
- Hold space for permanent teeth
- Guide proper jaw development
Early tooth loss can cause spacing and alignment problems later.

**When Is It Needed?**

Your child may need pulp therapy if they have:

- Deep cavities
- Ongoing tooth pain
- Swelling or abscess
- Tooth injury
Early treatment increases the chance of saving the tooth.

**Types of Pulp Therapy**

**1. Pulpotomy**
Removes infected pulp from the crown only (most common). Usually followed by a crown.

**2. Pulpectomy**
Removes all infected pulp from the crown and roots. The tooth is cleaned, filled, and restored with a crown.

Your pediatric dentist will choose the right option.

**Benefits**

- Relieves pain
- Stops infection
- Saves the natural tooth
- Prevents spacing problems
- Restores normal chewing

**Aftercare**

- Mild soreness is normal
- Avoid hard or sticky foods briefly
- Maintain good brushing habits
- Attend follow-up visits
Contact your dentist if swelling or severe pain occurs.
