**Braces & Clear Aligners: Straighten Your Smile**

A straight smile improves not only your appearance but also your oral health. Crooked or misaligned teeth can cause cleaning difficulties, jaw pain, and uneven wear. Braces and clear aligners provide effective orthodontic solutions for all ages.

**Why Teeth Straightening Matters**

✔ Improves smile aesthetics
✔ Enhances chewing function
✔ Makes cleaning easier
✔ Reduces risk of gum disease
✔ Prevents bite and jaw problems

Orthodontic treatment supports both confidence and long-term dental health.

**What Are Braces?**

Braces are fixed orthodontic appliances that gradually move teeth into proper alignment using gentle pressure.

**Metal Braces**

- Highly effective
- Ideal for complex cases
- Durable and reliable
Best suited for moderate to severe alignment issues.

**What Are Clear Aligners?**

Clear aligners are transparent, removable trays that straighten teeth discreetly. They are custom-made and replaced every few weeks to gradually shift teeth.

**Benefits of Clear Aligners**
✔ Nearly invisible
✔ Removable for eating and brushing
✔ Comfortable (no metal wires)
✔ Fewer clinic visits
✔ Best for mild to moderate cases

Popular among adults and working professionals.

**Braces vs Clear Aligners**

- **Braces:** Fixed, visible, ideal for complex cases
- **Aligners:** Removable, discreet, ideal for mild to moderate cases
An orthodontist will recommend the best option based on your condition.

**Treatment Process**

1. Consultation and digital scans
1. Personalized treatment plan
1. Braces placement or aligner fitting
1. Regular follow-ups
1. Retainers after completion
Treatment usually lasts **6–24 months**, depending on complexity.

**Are They Painful?**

Mild discomfort is normal initially, but modern techniques ensure safe and manageable treatment.

**Who Needs Orthodontic Treatment?**

You may benefit if you have:

- Crooked or crowded teeth
- Gaps
- Overbite or underbite
- Crossbite
- Jaw alignment issues
Early evaluation, especially for children and teens, helps prevent future complications.
