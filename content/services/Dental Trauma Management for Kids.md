**Dental Trauma Management for Kids**

Children are active, and falls, sports, and accidents can lead to dental injuries like chipped, broken, or knocked-out teeth. Quick action can help save the tooth and prevent complications.

**What Is Dental Trauma?**

Dental trauma is any injury to the teeth, gums, lips, or jaw caused by impact. It can affect both baby and permanent teeth.

**Common Injuries**

• Chipped or fractured tooth
• Knocked-out tooth
• Loose or displaced tooth
• Cracked tooth
• Cuts to lips, gums, or tongue

**What to Do in an Emergency**

**If a baby tooth is knocked out:**
• Do NOT reinsert it
• Control bleeding
• Visit a pediatric dentist immediately

**If a permanent tooth is knocked out:**
• Hold the tooth by the crown
• Rinse gently if dirty
• Reinsert if possible, or store in milk/saliva
• See a dentist within 30–60 minutes

**If a tooth is chipped or broken:**
• Rinse mouth
• Save fragments
• Apply cold compress
• Visit dentist as soon as possible

**Why Immediate Care Matters**

Delaying treatment can lead to infection, discoloration, or permanent damage. Early treatment improves success.

**Prevention Tips**

• Use mouth guards during sports
• Wear helmets during cycling
• Childproof your home
• Schedule regular dental checkups
